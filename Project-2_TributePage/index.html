Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

He was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology

Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J Kalam's life:

  • 1931: Born Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam on October 15, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955-1960: Studied Aerospace Engineering at Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1980: Played a key role in the successful launch of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which deployed the Rohini satellite.
  • 1980s-1990s: Served as the chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, leading the development of AGNI and PRITHVI missiles.
  • 1992-1999: Served as Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and Secretary of DRDO.
  • 1998: Played a key role in the Pokhran II nuclear tests
  • 2002-2007: Elected as the 11th President of India.
  • He passed away on July 27, 2015 after collapsing during a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management in Shillong.
